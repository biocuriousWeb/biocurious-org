# Mission

We believe that innovations in biology should be accessible, affordable, and open to everyone.

We’re building a community biology lab for amateurs, inventors, entrepreneurs, and anyone who wants to experiment and learn.

- A complete working laboratory and technical library for entrepreneurs to cheaply access equipment, materials, and co-working space
- A training center for biotechniques, with an emphasis on safety
- A meeting place for citizen scientists, hobbyists, activists, and students

{{< details title="Can I do my own project or research in the BioCurious Lab?" closed="true" >}}
If you want to use the BioCurious Lab for your personal project or research, you must first become a member. Click the red button on the upper right of the homepage and follow instructions. After you sign up, you will receive a welcome email that will tell you how to submit your project for safety committee review and how to take the safety test. Before working in the lab you must receive approval from the safety committee for your project and pass the safety test. BioCurious is a Biosafety Level 1 (BSL-1) lab. 
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Who is a Citizen Scientist?" closed="true" >}}
A Citizen Scientist is an amateur or non-professional scientist participating in scientific research. The goals of Citizen Science can include creating an open dialogue and collaboration between professional research and general public for the benefit of society. Research can often take place in a Makerspace or Hackerspace. 
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What is a Hackerspace / Makerspace?" closed="true" >}}
A <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hackerspace" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Hackerspace</a> / <a href="http://makerspace.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Makerspace</a> is a “community-operated workspace where people with common interests, often in computers, technology, science, digital art or electronic art, can meet, socialize and/or collaborate.”
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What is the Family Membership?" closed="true" >}}
Any person wanting to do lab work that is under 18 requires a family membership. A Family Membership is for 1 parent/guardian and 1 teen (under 18). Both the parent and the teen need to take the online safety training and pass the safety quiz. The project they are working on must be approved by the <a href="https://goo.gl/nSH2Pe">Safety Group</a>. Parents are required to be onsite and supervise their child anytime the teen is at BioCurious. 
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Isn’t allowing people to work in a biology lab dangerous?" closed="true" >}}
Our biology lab functions at a Biosafety Level – 1 level (BSL-1), which is equivalent to what you would find in a high school biology lab. We require all our members to undergo safety orientation.
